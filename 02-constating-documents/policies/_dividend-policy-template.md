## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the criteria and process for declaring and paying dividends to shareholders.

---

## 2. Decision Criteria

The Board may declare dividends when the following conditions are met:

### 2.1 Solvency Requirements

The Corporation must be able to meet both solvency tests under the Canada Business Corporations Act:
- Ability to pay liabilities as they become due
- Realizable value of assets exceeds liabilities plus stated capital

### 2.2 Financial Performance

Dividends will be considered when:
- [ ] Retained earnings exceed $[AMOUNT]
- [ ] Cash reserves exceed [NUMBER] months of operating expenses
- [ ] No outstanding obligations that would be materially affected

### 2.3 Business Needs

The Board shall consider:
- Capital requirements for planned investments
- Working capital needs
- Debt covenants and obligations
- Future funding requirements

---

## 3. Dividend Process

### 3.1 Declaration

1. Management prepares dividend recommendation with supporting financials
2. Board reviews and discusses the recommendation
3. Board passes a resolution to declare the dividend
4. Resolution specifies amount, record date, and payment date

### 3.2 Record Date

Shareholders of record as of the record date are entitled to receive the dividend.

### 3.3 Payment

Dividends shall be paid within [NUMBER] days of the record date by [cheque / wire transfer / other].

---

## 4. Types of Dividends

### 4.1 Cash Dividends

The primary form of dividend distribution.

### 4.2 Stock Dividends

May be considered when the Corporation wishes to conserve cash while rewarding shareholders.

---

## 5. Share Classes

Dividends shall be paid in accordance with the rights attached to each class of shares as set out in the Articles.

---

## 6. Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Board.
